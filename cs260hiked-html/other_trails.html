    Additional Trails

    Angel's Landing is a 4.3 mile out-and-back trail with 1827ft of elevation gain. It is considered challenging, especially for people with a fear of heights. Angel's Landing is extremely popular, so reservations are necessary to visit. Young children should not go on this trail because it has sheer cliffs. The views on this trail are unforgetable. Many say that this is the best trail in all of Zion's National Park.

    The Delicate Arch is a 3.2 mile out-and-back trail with 629ft of elevation gain. Famously, the Utah license plate holder proudly displays the Delicate Arch. It is widely considered a cultural icon of Utah. The trail is moderately diffucult and is suitable for most ages.

    King's Peak is the highest point in all of Utah. The most popular route to take is with Henry's Fork Trail. The total length is 25.0 miles with 4202ft of elevation gain. Therefore, most hikers will need more than one day to complete this challenging trail. Luckily, there are plenty of places to camp overnight. The views at the top are breathtaking, and the journey is unforgetable.
